01 · The problem

Children's most sensitive data is running through Big Tech.

Kindergartens run on WhatsApp groups and generic apps never built for them. Photos, health notes, allergies, where a child is at any hour: their entire day flows through platforms built to monetize attention and data.

⚠️

Sensitive data, wrong pipes

Photos, allergies, pick-up times and health notes pass through consumer apps designed to harvest data for ad targeting.

💬

No private line to the day

Parents have no dedicated, private channel to their child's educator. They get noisy group chats that mix logistics, photos and chit-chat.

🧩

Disconnected tools

Educators and directors juggle spreadsheets, paper sign-in sheets and a half-dozen apps that don't talk to each other.

02 · Proof it can be better

A whole country already proved parents want this.

1

Denmark built AULA

A dedicated national parent-educator platform. One channel for the whole relationship. No WhatsApp. Parents loved it, and it became how families and schools communicate nationwide.

2

But AULA runs on AWS

Even this flagship public platform sits on US-owned cloud infrastructure, and that became the problem.

3

The regulator refused

Denmark's own data authority, Datatilsynet, declined to approve the arrangement over fears that US authorities could compel access to children's data.

Amazon's guarantees that children's data is safe from US authorities were "pure theater."

David Heinemeier Hansson (creator of Ruby on Rails), on the AULA / AWS arrangement.
Reported by radar.dk, 2023.

The demand is proven. A genuinely sovereign version still doesn't exist.
